Use words that convey your feelings, and words that paint pictures for the reader. Be brief. Diary language has some typical features such as subjectless sentences (Got up late in the morning), sentence fragments without subjects or verbs (...too bad, boring, not good), contracted forms (they're, I ve, can t, didn t, etc.), and everyday expressions which people use in speech. Remember not to use such language in more formal kinds of writing.
