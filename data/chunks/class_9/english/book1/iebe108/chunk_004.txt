Simple Present Tense Study these sentences from the lesson. e A fight breaks out between two monkeys. e Film songs bilare out from the radios. e I wash it down with Coca-Cola. The italicised verbs are in the simple present tense. The writer is here describing what he saw and heard but he uses the present tense instead of the past tense. A narration or a story can be made more dramatic or immediate by using the present tense in this way. Now look at the following sentences. e A small shrine half protrudes from the stone platform on the riverbank. e Small shops stand on the outer edge of the Stupa. We use the simple present tense to speak about what is usually or generally true. The sentences above describe facts. We also use the simple present tense in sentences depicting universal truths . For example: e The sun rises in the east. e The earth revolves round the sun. We can also refer to habitual actions using the simple present tense. e He usually takes a train instead of a bus to work. e We often get fine drizzles in winter.
