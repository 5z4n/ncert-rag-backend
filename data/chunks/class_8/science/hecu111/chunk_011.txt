The year begins on 22 March, which is the day after the spring equinox. Unlike the Gregorian calendar, months in the Indian National Calendar have either 30 or 31 days. In 1952, the Government of India set up a Calendar Reform Committee (CRC) to examine all existing calendars which were being followed in the country at that time and to recommend an accurate and uniform calendar for the whole of India. The CRC recommended Unified National Calendar was adopted for use with effect from 21 March 1956 CE, that is, 1 Chaitra 1878 Saka. The Indian National Calendar follows the general principles as that of the Surya Siddhanta. Meghnad Saha (1893-1956) Meghnad Saha was a pioneering astrophysicist of India who studied stars and their temperatures and developed a mathematical equation, famously known as the Saha equation.
